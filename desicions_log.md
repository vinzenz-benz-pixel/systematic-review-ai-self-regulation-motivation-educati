Hybrid Intelligence and the Support of Self-Regulation
Hybrid intelligence places a strong emphasis on the mechanisms through which artificial intelligence (AI) can support self-regulation in educational contexts. In recent years, AI has attracted considerable research attention as a means of fostering students’ self-regulated learning. However, despite its growing potential, AI also exhibits substantial limitations. Consequently, teachers will continue to play an essential role in educational settings.
A central challenge lies in the appropriate distribution of functions, roles, and tasks between teachers and AI systems. The existing literature discusses various patterns of responsibility-sharing between human teachers and AI-based tools. The present review focuses on identifying and systematizing these distribution patterns (see Ninaus & Sailer, 2022).

Models of Self-Regulation
To address the wide range of strategies and methods used to support self-regulation, a comprehensive framework model is required. An integrative framework for self-regulated learning has recently been proposed by Wortha et al. (2026), as reported in Fütterer et al. (2026). A key strength of this model is its interdisciplinary approach, which integrates insights from cognitive science, personality psychology, educational psychology, and psychology more broadly (Fütterer et al., 2026).
